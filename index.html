  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Gradually add warm water and knead into a smooth, soft dough.
  2. Add 2 tablespoons of oil and knead again for 5-7 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  3. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes to 1 hour.
  4. Divide the dough into equal-sized balls. Roll out one ball into a thin circle on a floured surface.
  5. Brush the rolled dough lightly with oil, then sprinkle a pinch of flour evenly over it.
  6. Starting from one edge, fold the dough into pleats, then roll the pleated dough into a round coil, tucking the end under.
  7. Flatten the coil gently and roll it out again into a 6-8 inch circle, keeping the layers intact.
  8. Heat a skillet over medium heat. Place the rolled chapati on the hot skillet.
  9. Cook for about 30 seconds or until you see bubbles forming, then flip. Brush the cooked side lightly with oil or ghee.
  10. Flip again and press gently with a spatula, cooking until golden brown spots appear and the chapati puffs up in layers.
  11. Remove from heat and keep warm in a covered container. Repeat with the remaining dough balls.

üí° Tips for Perfect Soft Layered Chapatis

  • Make sure the dough is soft but not sticky for easy rolling.
  • Use oil or ghee between layers to get distinct flaky layers.
  • Cook on medium heat to avoid burning and ensure even cooking.
  • Keep cooked chapatis covered to retain moisture and softness.
